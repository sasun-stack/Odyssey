# KP Tickets — design rules

## Color
- **NEVER use cream / warm beige backgrounds.** This applies to page backgrounds, card backgrounds, ticket-paper backgrounds — anywhere. Specifically avoid tones like `#EDE8DE`, `#F6EFDF`, `#EBE2CB`, `#F5EBD6`, or anything that reads as "old paper / cream".
- For light mode, use **neutral light grays / off-whites** (e.g. `#F2F2F2`, `#F4F4F4`, pure `#FFFFFF` for cards). The KinoPark brand is black + warm orange (`#F26B1F`) — keep light surfaces clean and neutral, not warm-tinted.
- Accent color is `#F26B1F` (KP orange). Use sparingly for time / CTA / key highlights.

## Ticket-stub variant
- Use **vertical** orientation (poster top, perforation across middle, QR stub at bottom). Never horizontal.
- No rotation / tilt on the ticket body.
- Do **not** show movie title twice (don't repeat it on the poster overlay if it already appears below).
- On the QR stub, show information **useful to a cashier at the entrance**: ticket count ("Admit N"), concession items (so they can hand over snacks), age rating. The order number is for support — keep it small/secondary, not as the prominent stub field.
